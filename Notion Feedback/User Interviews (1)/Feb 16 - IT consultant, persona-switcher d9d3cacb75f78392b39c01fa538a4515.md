# Feb 16 - IT consultant, persona-switcher

Interview Date: February 16, 2026

Key Themes: Comparison to therapy, Pacing feedback, Voice/audio feedback, Wants expertise
Paid v. Organic: Paid

User Description: Male, 30-40, IT consultant between jobs. Using Ash for 6 months, recently engaging more deeply with persona modes. Interested in psychology degree focused on organizational development/high-performance athletes.

11:45am, 2.16.26

## Intro: background + why he started using Ash

- Male between 30-40 years old; works as an IT consultant but is currently between jobs
- Has been using Ash for six months - shared that he has "been digging into it much more on a daily basis" now that it has "new modes."
    - Not the first user to share that our persona presets have helped them engage with Ash in a deeper or more productive way!

## Persona-related feedback

- Switches between Scholar and Coach mode depending on his needs.
    - Uses Coach when he is at "a fork in a road" - "It's not that Ash tells me what to choose… but it teaches me how to think about what to choose."
    - Uses Scholar mode to get more of a birds-eye view - for example, to understand why he's ending up in certain patterns or why he's overanalyzing certain things.
    - "I find a lot of things fun and don't know where to put my focus, so a lot of things feel half-done… after having discussions with Ash, it helps me work in parallel lanes" and approach things from less of an all-or-nothing standpoint.
        - Cited his job search and a new interest in creating cooking content on Instagram as an example of "parallel lanes."
- What motivated him to explore the new personas feature - dissatisfaction with Classic Ash, or just a general sense of curiosity?
    - "In the beginning, [Classic Ash] wasn't serving me in the way I wanted… it was more of a check-in. With the new modes the feedback feels more concrete and tangible."
    - However, since the new modes have been implemented, he observed that "it's talking a little bit too much - it should give the information, but it should pause a little bit and wait for confirmation or feedback from the user."
        - What does he mean by confirmation or feedback, I asked?
            - "It's like it's talking… and when it starts talking it's talking and talking… and during that time I have time to formulate my thoughts and questions. But since it doesn't stop, I forget the questions [that I was going to ask]."
            - This is not the first time I've heard a user share that foundation model verbosity isn't an ideal experience for them!
        - Emphasized that verbosity isn't the biggest issue: "It's a luxury problem that it does this… it's not like it broken."

## Exploratory questions re: a potential new social feature

- Are you involved in any online social forums, like Reddit or Facebook? And if so, do you ever share personal or sensitive content?
    - "I have FB, Instagram, and TikTok… what I share on that is basically cooking and travel. With, LinkedIn I'm a very bad user… I'm not the kind of user who shares, like, my life is going on in a rollercoaster at the moment."
        - When he shares stuff on social media, it normally tends to be focused on year-end highlights, good memories, or wins (positives vs. struggles).
    - "Being in forums like Quora, Reddit, Substack… writing about stuff like, this is how you set up your goals, this is what you do when you're experiencing lows or highs… I don't do that."
        - Overall, this user didn't seem to have much of an appetite for self-improvement or mental health-related sharing.
- Do you find yourself drawn to other people sharing those kinds of vulnerable stories?
    - "No, but I get a lot of people coming to me when they have challenges in life, like close friends."
        - It seems like he engages with vulnerable topics/sharing more in real life vs. on the internet.
- Would you be interested in knowing what other Ash users are going through?
    - Prefaced his answer by saying he's interested in doing a 5-year degree program in psychology, but he'd specifically want to learn about organizational development + how to support high-performance athletes.
    - Friends in the field have warned him that it's tough to work with people who have experienced trauma; said it's given him pause re: pursuing the degree, because "I'm not sure I can take that on."
        - Went on to say, for that reason, that "it depends" on what kind of content people would be sharing. I took that to mean that if folks were sharing a lot of traumatic or intense stuff in a social forum, he wouldn't be as willing to engage.
            - I can imagine that other users might feel similarly, so as we observe and potentially filter the posts that are going up, we should perhaps keep in mind that we might want to avoid triggering or re-traumatizing people.
- If you could envision a social feature within Ash that you might want to use, what would it look like? I encouraged him not to place any limits on his thinking, and share any type of "pie in the sky" vision that comes to mind.
    - "I like it very much as it is right now… I don't know what the social thing would add to it."
    - Shared that he talks to Ash about things he doesn't really talk to his friends about, "so I don't know how I would like to socialize with other people using Ash, or what we would be talking about in my case… there might be other people who are more open, and would like that."
    - I then asked: is there a reason why you feel more comfortable sharing certain things with Ash vs. other people in your life?
        - "When you tell people things you're getting a view of the world through their eyes… when I talk to Ash I'm getting non-biased feedback from a computer… it helps me get to know myself because it doesn't know me."
            - I followed up: and that's not something you feel you could get from another person, whether it's a stranger or a friend?
                - "Usually, when I talk to someone I measure… what's my gut feeling when I leave a conversation. Sometimes you don't get a good gut feeling… when that happens too often, I need to step back and think about whether these conversations should continue."
                - With Ash, the gut feeling is mostly good… because I can go back to it with the same problem 100 times. If I got to a friend or a therapist with the same problem 100 times, they'll get fed up, or they'll just book me for more sessions."
        - I think it's important to note that a lot of people are drawn to Ash for this reason - its privacy/lack of judgment is part of the appeal. Perhaps this is not the subset of users we should be targeting or trying to accommodate with this feature - it seems pretty antithetical to what Ash gives them/what they are looking for.

## Final piece of feedback

- Has noticed some issues with audio mode (he usually speaks to Ash out loud). However, these are relatively infrequent, and he figured it's probably due to his network connection.
    - "The times that it's working, the feedback it gives [outweighs the audio issues]."
