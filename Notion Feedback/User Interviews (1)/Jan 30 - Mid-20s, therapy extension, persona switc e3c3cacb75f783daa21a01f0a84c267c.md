# Jan 30 - Mid-20s, therapy extension, persona switcher

Interview Date: January 30, 2026
Interviewer: Clare Flanagan
Key Themes: Comparison to therapy, Prefers directness, Wants expertise
Paid v. Organic: Paid
Primary Persona: The Real One
User Description: Female in mid-20s, found Ash on TikTok but didn't try until struggling with mental health. Uses Ash in combination with traditional therapy as "extension to therapy" and journaling tool that prompts deeper exploration. Has chronic illness and disability. First user interviewed who routinely switches between personas.

## Intro - background info + why she started using Ash

- Female, Mid-20s
- Initially stumbled upon it through TikTok, but didn't give it a try until months after, when she was struggling with her mental health quite a lot.
- Uses Ash in combination with traditional therapy
    - "It's like an extension to therapy, kind of. I use it as a journal. Instead of writing out my thoughts it prompts me to dig deeper… it helps me use my therapy time [more productively]."
    - She mentioned that Ash helps her prioritize/dig into topics she wouldn't otherwise during her in-person therapy sessions.
- Like other users, values immediate/in-the-moment access to help, especially as it applies to emotion regulation skills.
    - "When I'm struggling on my own, it narrates and guides me through using DBT skills, which I sometimes find hard [to do independently]."

## Persona-specific feedback

- Has tried them, but didn't notice "that big of a difference" between personas.
- Is there one that she prefers?
    - "There's one that does a good job with the DBT skills over the other ones. When I'm struggling I switch it to that. It used to give me the same skill over and over, but now it kind of adapts to what I need in the moment."
        - Interestingly, this persona turned out to be Coach, and not our DBT-specific persona!!
- "I did notice another one that is kind of direct… that's kind of how I like things. I need things a little straightforward… I don't need things sugarcoated. Sometimes you want to hear things validated, but you also want to hear the other side."
- Overall, reflected that the personas have helped her "dig deeper" than the previous, one-note version of Ash did. (Yay!)

## Descriptive copy feedback

- Had to go back to the in-app menu to recall the copy, and then remembered that Coach was the persona that she's been using for DBT skills.
    - Interesting, because the copy does not specifically describe it as being capable of DBT.
- "I haven't tried the scholar… I didn't really like the bestie one just because it's not very me or in line with my personality. I do see a very good use for it for someone else."
- "The one that I use the most is "The Real One."

## Persona switching behavior

- Important to note - this is the first user I've spoken to who routinely switches back and forth between personas. I asked her what that experience is like - is there anything disjointed or frustrating about the process of switching?
    - "In an ideal world you wouldn't have to switch between different ones. It would kind of do everything. But [the personas] allow me to do what I want in the moment and get what I need."
    - "It's not an inconvenience to switch - it doesn't feel disjointed. The conversation still feels continuous."
    - Very interesting feedback, because she seemed to have a distinct preference for pre-set personas and a lack of interest in building a fully customized Ash.
        - More evidence on this front: when asked about her "Dream Ash," she said, "I'm not a very imaginative person, so this isn't a good question for me."
        - However... if we had a good enough customization process, could she build an Ash that flexibly does all the things that her preferred personas do?
        - In any case, there's an opportunity here to reduce friction for people who like to persona-hop - she mentions that she sometimes "forgets to switch," so I wonder how we could solve that problem.

## Dream Ash / Frustrations

- Because the Dream Ash question was not productive, I asked instead - is there anything you've found frustrating?
    - "There were a couple days where it wasn't loading properly - a big part of it was my internet."
    - "I've recently been starting to get used to using AI a little more, so I'm still trying to understand what it's capable of. I don't really understand what "the sky is the limit" means…" (in response to my encouragement to imagine her ideal persona with no boundaries or limits on the traits she could customize!)
        - Could this be an indicator that customization could be too overwhelming for people who aren't as familiar with AI? We'll work to make the experience accessible for everyone.

## Closing thoughts: how has your Ash experience differed from traditional therapy?

- "It doesn't always understand depression well - I get that little blurb about calling 988 a lot [because] I have a lot of passive suicidal ideation… but when I'm talking to my therapist, the safety plan doesn't come up much because she understands I'm not actually in that space mentally."
    - This user seemed to understand why Ash does this (because it prioritizes safety). But it still indicates that there's an opportunity to improve the crisis experience for people who are not in immediate danger.
- Reiterated that "the immediacy makes a really big difference" - she appreciates having in-the-moment access to Ash when she really needs it.
- "Talking to a human is different from talking to an AI… it tries its best to understand, but an AI can't really do that."
    - I then asked: how does your therapist help you feel understood in ways that Ash can't?
        - She responded: "I have chronic illness and disability and so does my therapist. Having that connection matters, especially when she self-discloses… Ash can't do that because it's not disabled. It can't always understand life because it doesn't have that experience, although it has a decent grasp of what life is like with disability and chronic illness, which is pretty interesting."
        - Good insight from Raahil - "AI too does have very clear disabilities! Maybe Ash being vulnerable about its own shortcomings and not presenting as an all knowing AI would help with working alliance!"
- Overall, ended on a positive note - "I will continue using it." : )
