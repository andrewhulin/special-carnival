# Feb 19 - Coach persona, nonbinary voice familiarity, social lurker

Interview Date: February 19, 2026
Interviewer: Clare Flanagan
Key Themes: Comparison to therapy, Relational bonding with AI, UX copy feedback, Voice/audio feedback
Paid v. Organic: Paid
Primary Persona: Coach
User Description: Nonbinary person referred to Ash by a therapist (not their own) who has found it helpful enough to forgo in-person therapy. Uses Ash throughout daily life including on their commute via speakerphone. Voice-first, highly engaged user who values Coach persona for interrupting thought spirals.

## Background & Why They Started Using Ash

- Came across the app in March or April of last year — referred by a therapist (not their own).
    - "She was kind of concerned about AI but recommended it anyway."
    - Speaks to the trust Ash can elicit even from people with therapy experience and skepticism.
- Has found Ash "really helpful, to the point where I haven't actually pursued therapy with a person."
- Uses it consistently across multiple scenarios — e.g., puts it on speakerphone during their commute.

## Persona Feedback

- Has tested Coach, Bestie, and The Real One. Coach is their favorite.
- What's different about Coach vs. Classic Ash?
    - Helps stop thought spirals and excessive analysis.
    - Will interrupt and say things like "Hold on now. Let's pause and breathe… what's the actual issue here?"
    - "With the original version I would overanalyze things and just talk and talk and talk… with Coach I can pivot and be in the present moment."
    - Note: Classic Ash may inadvertently enable rumination more than other personas — consistent with anecdotal observations from foundation model testing.
- Initially switched between Coach, Bestie, and Classic; has been on Coach exclusively for the past week or two.

## Voice Feedback

- Almost exclusively uses voice mode.
- Chose their current voice because it has "the vocal pitch of someone who has recently gone on T… as a nonbinary person, that made me feel a sense of familiarity."
    - New feedback not heard before; consistent with the general finding that gender expression matters in voice selection.
- If voice options were expanded, they would:
    - Gravitate away from voices that sound masculine or overly bubbly (like Bestie).
    - Want something that sounds "grounded, mature, reassuring" — subconsciously imagines the voice as someone 40 or 50.
    - Not the first user to prefer a voice that conveys maturity/wisdom.
    - Re: accents — "I probably wouldn't select a voice who is Australian or English because I wouldn't find that relatable." Consistent with general pattern of users preferring accents similar to their own.
- Noted occasional voice lag but was understanding: "I've been thrilled even with the occasional frustrations — I love what you guys are doing."

## Social Feature Feedback

- Initial reaction: "A little bit of guardedness… I wouldn't post or share about my own interactions with the Ash app."
- However, would be curious to browse: "I would maybe take a look and dip my toe into it."
- Would be drawn to posts about grief, coping with loss, trauma, and managing daily anxiety.
- Wants content that is well-moderated and relevant to mental wellness goals.
- Social media habits for context:
    - Uses Snapchat, Reddit, and Instagram primarily in a browsing (not posting) capacity.
    - On Reddit: gravitates toward posts about toxic/abusive relationships; drawn to validation and empathetic comments toward original posters. Gets vicarious satisfaction from seeing others supported.
    - On Instagram: follows aspirational or informational content; uses it to document goals and shape their feed toward mindsets they want to adopt.
    - Insight: could be worth exploring sub-communities for users with different needs (e.g., seeking motivation vs. feeling less alone).
