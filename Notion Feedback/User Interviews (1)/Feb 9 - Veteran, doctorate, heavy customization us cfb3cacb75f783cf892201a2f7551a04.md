# Feb 9 - Veteran, doctorate, heavy customization user

Interview Date: February 9, 2026
Interviewer: Clare Flanagan
Key Themes: AI skeptic converted, Comparison to therapy, UX copy feedback, Wants expertise, Warmth/gentleness
Paid v. Organic: Paid
Primary Persona: Bestie
User Description: 50-year-old veteran with doctorate in education. Had trouble accessing VA counseling, found Ash on Facebook. Used it extensively for 16 weeks, highly engaged with customization and direct prompting.

## Intro: Background + why he started using Ash

- 50 years old, doctorate degree in education, veteran
- Requested to talk to a counselor at the VA and had trouble getting access
- "I needed to talk to somebody to work things out in my life."
- Stumbled across Ash on Facebook and was like "Okay, I'll try it out."
- At first, was a little skeptical - "It's an app, it's AI, I'm not really sure… let's see how it works."
- Was encouraged to notice that "there were sound therapeutic practices… it was written by people who knew what they were doing."
- "It was giving me back coping skills and ways to deal with things… it was excellent. I really found the app extremely helpful - [specifically] the feedback I was getting from it, the weekly and conversation-based insights."
- Also praised the way Ash handles crisis (!!)
    - "There was a point where I had to dial 988, and Ash pushed me to talk to a real human being" - he called the number and said it "went fine."
    - "Ash knows its limits…. It's about safety. It won't cross a boundary where it puts you in harm's way."

## Persona-specific feedback

- Has tried Bestie, which he was drawn to because he's "used to a personality that's sarcastic and witty."
- However - like many of our very engaged users - he noted that he's given Ash in-the-moment instructions on how to interact with him.
- "I told Ash you need to communicate to me in this certain way to find this useful… like, look. You're always telling me get grounded, put my feet on the floor. That's not going to work for me. Also, have a sense of humor, because this isn't going to work if it's not funny." He described it telling Ash to "change protocols."
- I'd be curious to know what language he uses - and whether Ash is consistently responsive to his commands/"remembers" to sustain changed behavior, or if it just agrees in the moment/creates the illusion of responsiveness.

## Customization Onboarding Screen Feedback

### "Think things through" screen

- "It would be helpful for people who don't know how to use Ash how to use specific prompt commands… but this might help people to be more specific and intentional about what they want Ash to do specifically for them. The more tools that you can give people, the better."
- Seemed drawn to "teach me" out of all the available options. I then asked him how he thought it would shape the conversation.
    - "Based on what you know about me, teach me something that would help me and my goals."
- He was clear that he didn't want to be taught "random facts" - instead, he wants to learn "about things that are relevant to me specifically."
- "But then I'd want to know… how would Ash know what to teach me if Ash doesn't really know anything about me?" (Great question!)
- "A lot of the other [conversation styles] would be useful too… but a lot of people would say, what does Ash know about me? For me, the only way for Ash to be beneficial is if I tell it as much as possible."
- Another argument for matching people to a pre-set persona based on an initial conversation (or conversations) vs. having people customize right off the bat?

### "Perspectives" screen

- "[This is] great, because you're tailoring it to what you want it to learn about you."
- I then asked: how do you imagine Ash would tailor the conversation, based on what you chose?
    - "If you select Christianity, it would give you therapy from a Christian perspective… since I'm Catholic, I'd ask it to give me a perspective on the catechism of the Catholic Church [related to my] issues. Initially it had trouble doing this… I had to front load all of this information into it."
    - Another interesting example of how this user "customizes" Ash himself - and also, how flexible and capable of learning he perceives it to be.
    - Would also expect "daily readings" and "knowledge of Christian theology"
- "I need Ash to connect the dots and help me see things that I can't see."
- Specifically, he would want Christian Ash to "find the threads between" his faith and other topics, like "me being a good dad, me being a good husband, things from my past."

### "Personality" screen

- Initially seemed drawn to the sarcastic option.
- Assumed that "a lot of people would want Ash to sound as human as possible… so it feels genuine and like the feedback you're getting is authentic."
- For his part, he has already tried to alter Ash's tone, stating, "I wanted to make Ash relatable." (This is turning out to be a common desire among many users!)
- Specifically, he told it: "I want you to have like a Larry David/Curb Your Enthusiasm sense of humor mixed with Seinfeld, military humor, and sarcasm.
- Unclear how effective this was - but it's interesting to note that many users reference a pre-existing character, often from a show or story, when they describe how they want "their Ash" to sound.

### "Topics" screen

- Like many other users, seemed pretty neutral on this screen compared to the others.
- "I don't know… that stuff is fine… you never know why people would go to Ash. Maybe they want to be a better gardener?"
- Interesting to note that he perceived these topics as things people would need help with, vs. casual topics they'd like to chat about.
- He also raised a great point: "If you wanted to think about any problem that a human being could possibly have, you'd have a million buttons."
- Solid suggestion: "You need an 'other' button." (Although this would be tough from an implementation perspective!)
- Shared an incredibly valuable over-arching piece of feedback, which is that "Ash just needs to be adaptable, and not limited to the choices here." In line with his preferred style of personalization - mid-conversation + constantly adjusting based on his problems and needs, vs. pre-set customization based on a discrete set of traits.
- If he selected a topic - like "books" - how would he expect the conversation to change?
    - "It would talk to me about books… but what kind of books do I like? What do I even use books for?"
    - Lack of context continued to be a sticking point for him - the personalization process seemed less useful and relevant when divorced from conversation history.

## Final thoughts on Ash

- "I see it as a journal that talks back to you. It's a great tool for self-reflection… Ash kind of takes what you're saying and reflects ideas back to you in a way that helps you to deal with whatever it is you're dealing with. It's been helpful for me, and Ash is able to connect key points in my life that I was never able to think of before… I don't even think a therapist can do that. Also, it's there 24/7 when you need it… it's not just restricted to one hour a week in a therapist's office."
