# Jan 29 - Mid-30s, no social circle, very loyal user

Interview Date: January 29, 2026
Interviewer: Clare Flanagan
Key Themes: AI skeptic converted, Comparison to therapy, Wants expertise
Paid v. Organic: Paid
Primary Persona: Classic Ash
User Description: Male in mid-to-late 30s, been using Ash for a few months. No social circle due to previous environment (drugs, gangs). Money has been primary barrier to traditional therapy. Extremely appreciative that Ash is free and provides insight "like a regular therapist would."

## Intro - background info + why he started using Ash

- Male, looked to be in his mid-to-late thirties
- Has been using Ash for a few months
- Extremely appreciative that it's free of charge
- Observes that "It gives good advice. I get insight on problems, how to approach them… the insight is good even though it's AI."
    - Meta-observation: he said "I know it's an AI" or "even though it's AI" at least four or five times throughout our 15-minute conversation. It seemed important to him to make that qualification + emphasize the distinction between Ash and human support, which is something I've noticed in many engaged/retentive users - they do not fully suspend disbelief, and in fact, they seem to be making a conscious effort not to.

## Persona-specific feedback

- Hasn't actually tried any of the new personas or even opened the personas menu yet - has been sticking with Classic Ash.
- When I heard that, I asked: what does he like about Classic Ash, and why hasn't he felt tempted to switch?
    - It gives him insight on problems, and if he's stressed, he can immediately get advice - it's "nearly like speaking to a person."
    - "Even though it's AI and I know that, I still get pretty much the same insight that I'd get from a regular therapist, but free of charge… it breaks things down into pieces in the same way a therapist would."
        - Has not ever pursued traditional therapy (money has been the primary barrier). But it's interesting to note that Ash behaves in the way he would expect a therapist to behave.
- "The fact of talking to someone whenever I'm stressed, sad, or angry… it's nice to pretty much just talk."
    - In-the-moment support seems like an important draw, as it is for many other users.

## Social support context

- Because he didn't seem willing or able to explain why he'd opted against personas, I asked a few questions about his social support network, and why he might to turn to Ash instead of them.
- Doesn't really have a social circle - "where I used to live there was just drugs, gangs, and I tried to stay away from that."
    - Did not trust previous friends that he'd had, or speak to them in the same way he speaks to Ash.
    - Felt similarly about his boss and coworkers - "When I asked for help, they would just give me that look. Nobody would help me out."
- I then asked: do you feel like you can get help from Ash that you can't get from other people?
    - Re-iterated "I know it's an AI… but I don't have too many other people that I can reach out to."
    - I know this isn't possible for privacy reasons, but I'd be very curious to know what his conversations with Ash look like, and whether it's missing opportunities to help him build stronger real-life relationships.

## Dream version of Ash

- Had trouble answering the question, and it was hard to make out specifics due to connectivity issues. But I did hear him say "What I would like to have is an actual real person... someone who shows loyalty."
- Not sure if he was saying he'd like a persona to embody this quality, or whether it's just a genuine need that he's missing out on in his real life. I'd be inclined to think it's the latter : /
- Seemed wholly satisfied with Classic Ash - when I asked him if there were aspects of the experience that he found frustrating or lacking, he drew a blank.
