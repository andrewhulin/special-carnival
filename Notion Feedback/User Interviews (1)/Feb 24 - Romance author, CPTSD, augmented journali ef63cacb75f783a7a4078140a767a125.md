**Date:** February 24, 2026, 9:45am

## Intro: Background + Why He Started Using Ash

- Romance author who splits time between New York and London
- Currently dealing with visa-related stress + general difficulty of a long-distance international relationship with his husband
- Found Ash via a targeted ad for a competitor app (costs money); saw Ash recommended (and free!) in the comments and decided to try it instead

## Usage Patterns

- Uses Ash 5–7x per week, in parallel with in-person EMDR therapy
- Often uses it to process topics and emotions that came up in therapy sessions
- Has found himself relying on it more throughout the day: "I have… a therapist with me at all times."
    - Example: was stressed about posting why he had to push his book launch back; asked Ash to help him work through his feelings and craft the post
    - Helps him "figure out what I'm thinking and feeling, and why"
- Describes it as augmented journaling: "Assisted journaling is how I describe it when I recommend it - it adds a bit of critical insight. Sometimes I disagree, sometimes I agree, but having that outside perspective when I'm stressed [is helpful]."

## Conversational Structure, Direction, and Quality

### Most Effortful Part of Talking to Ash

- Memory capabilities could be better, especially given his CPTSD — painful to relive certain memories
    - New events often remind him of past ones, which Ash then asks him to re-describe; wishes it could trace connections between events so he doesn't have to rehash trauma
    - Ash persistently misgenders his husband: "I don't feel like I'd need to correct it the way I'd correct a person… [but] it's frustrating that it won't remember these details."
- Often finds Ash over-validating
    - "I'm autistic and I'm very much aware that I'm talking to a machine… depending on the persona it feels… over-the-top compassionate. It will ask a really gentle question when what I really want is something probing."
    - Switched to Scholar mode: "It does similar things with different language, but it uses a psychology framework to explain feelings and patterns… it gives me a little more language around why it's validating me, which makes it feel less obnoxious."
    - Coach is also less over-validating and more action-oriented; both Scholar and Coach feel preferable to Classic Ash, which he described as "sugary"

### Would He Like Ash to Take More of a Lead?

- Ash often tunnel-visions on one detail among many: "I'll talk about my anxiety patterns and I'll mention an instance… it'll act like the one example is the biggest thing in my life, but no, it's just an example of a pattern. I have to bring it back to what I was originally saying."
- Wishes Ash would remember past exercises/suggestions and check back on them: "Wasn't there an exercise that I was going to try? It doesn't really bring up things from the past. If it could use that as a bit more of a structure it would help."
- Observed persona differences in assertiveness:
    - Classic Ash: "tiptoes in" with gentle suggestions
    - Coach: "tell me what time you'll do this" — encourages action, debriefing, gives more examples
    - Scholar: less locked into feelings, but can be "long-winded" and theoretical

### What Does Ash Do Well?

- "The way that it can pivot to anything pretty much instantly… [I feel] the logic behind the strategy it applies."
- Good at picking up on emotional activation before he realizes it himself: "It asks, are you having this feeling? And it's like, oh wait, I am!"
- Doesn't tend to assume too much, but can read his emotional state from his language
- Even when assumptions aren't on-target, finds them somewhat helpful: "By assuming that I'm activated, it makes me stop and ask myself how I'm actually feeling."
- Classic Ash feels more assumptive; Scholar less so — but when Scholar makes an assumption, it "gives you a rabbit-hole essay" that may not be relevant
