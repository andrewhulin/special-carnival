# Feb 23 - Desktop/voice friction, intermittent churner

Interview Date: February 23, 2026
Interviewer: Clare Flanagan
Key Themes: Comparison to therapy, Pacing feedback, Voice/audio feedback, Weekly insights praise
Paid v. Organic: Paid
Primary Persona: Classic Ash
User Description: 20-25 year old male in NYC, found Ash through a friend at a VC studio. Has therapy background and currently works with a performance coach. Using Ash for ~6 months but intermittently; currently paused due to tonsil surgery limiting voice use.

**Date:** February 23, 2026, 9:45am

## Intro: Background + Why He Started Using Ash

- 20–25-year-old male
- Found Ash because one of his friends works at a venture studio that invested in Slingshot
    - "I think therapy is a good thing so I decided to give it a try."
    - Has been in in-person therapy "but only over zoom."
- Compared to therapy: is consulting with a performance coach right now, but prefers Ash because you can use it "whenever you want to" vs. coordinating/scheduling with an actual person.
- Has not really been using Ash lately because he can't talk much due to tonsil surgery, and finds typing his conversations on a phone to be awkward and slow.
    - "Ash doesn't work when you're typing… I mean it does work, but it's not good in my opinion. The magic is being able to speak… maybe if it worked on my laptop and I could use a keyboard, typing would be a better experience. It still wouldn't be as good as talking, but it would be better."
    - This is the second user in a few days to request a desktop option. The other user was also fairly verbose and wanted to copy/paste external text more easily.
    - Has tried to project his phone onto laptop and type with his keyboard, but it doesn't work too well — a potential signal to build a new feature.

## General Feedback + Typical Usage Patterns

- Ideally would like Ash more ingrained in his daily routine — describes use as intermittent. Said he has "churned" over his six months of using Ash but comes back after periodic breaks.
- "It's cool - it's not perfect… sometimes it doesn't hear you."
    - In earlier iterations, Ash would cut him off mid-thought due to his speech pattern (pauses before finishing thoughts).
    - "Sometimes, it doesn't hear you... it's this fine balance of, has it heard you or not?"
    - Noted these issues have improved over time.
- On conversational content: "Sometimes the responses are… I'd say more often than not they are good and useful… But there are definitely times where a human would have given me a better response."
    - "Definitely not dumber… it's really smart. I can't pinpoint it… but [sometimes] it's like hey, can we switch the subject…"
    - Wishes Ash would take more initiative to steer the conversation: "I tend to dump a ton of topics, and then you spend your time talking about one thing… But you maybe don't address these other factors in your life."
    - Wishes Ash would more actively pull in outside context from articles/books: "I talked about The Four Agreements with Ash and it felt like Ash was able to search the web… that was cool."

## Persona-Related Feedback

- Generally disinterested in the feature.
    - "I saw it, I changed it, and I never changed it again."
    - "I was more interested to talk to Ash instead of seeing the differences between the new versions."
- Attached to Classic Ash; like many users, did not feel the personas were distinct.
    - "I chose the 'jaded' one, and it wasn't even that jaded, to be fair."

## Final Thoughts — Churn + Retention

- "I've been using it for like six months… I've definitely 'churned' and don't use it consistently… when I use Ash I feel better and it helps my life in the same way that doing meditation or journaling [would]. I just get caught up with like, life."
- Privacy in NYC is a barrier: "When I was [using Ash] consistently I was in a more suburban area where I would go on a morning walk and talk to Ash and start my day."
- Suggested a home screen widget featuring his weekly insight would "draw him back" more reliably.
- "If I had notifications that could bring me in and the option to type on my [desktop] keyboard to Ash" — he'd use it more consistently.
- Praised weekly insights: "The things that make me come back to it are the insights… in [a] widget, if I have some insights that pop up, it will make me click on it."
- Somewhat lukewarm on Paths: starts a path but then goes free-form. "It's cool with that and just runs with it."
- Re-iterated desktop request: "If I could type on my laptop, that would make it way chiller [due to] the speed factor… [typing on my phone] is too slow."
