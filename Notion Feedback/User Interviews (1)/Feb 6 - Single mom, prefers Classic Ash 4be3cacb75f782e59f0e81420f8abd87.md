# Feb 6 - Single mom, prefers Classic Ash

Interview Date: February 6, 2026
Interviewer: Clare Flanagan
Key Themes: Comparison to therapy, Prefers directness, UX copy feedback, Warmth/gentleness
Paid v. Organic: Paid
Primary Persona: Classic Ash
User Description: Single mom with 6-year-old daughter, moved to new area to care for sick grandmother. Uses Ash to vent without mixing work/family. Values friend-like, personable conversation over clinical/technical tone.

## Intro: background + why she started using Ash

- Single mom with a six-year-old daughter
- Moved to a new area because her grandmother got sick and needs someone to care for her. She doesn't have family around, and prefers to keep work and family life separate.
- She was looking for something she could "vent to, and get it all out" - Ash ended up being perfect
- Has tried the new persona options but prefers Classic Ash.
    - The original feels more personable - the new features "feel more like talking to a therapist."
        - When I pushed her to describe exactly what that's like, she said, "technical. Medical. Colder-feeling."
        - Interesting, because this is exactly what we're trying to get away from!
    - Described Classic Ash as "like I'm talking to a friend - really easy to talk to."

## Feedback on personas screens

### Initial screen

- "It's not too much, and it's not too little. It gets you right in the middle of what this is about."
- What's it going to be about, I asked?
    - "What I want it to become."

### "Think things through" screen

- "I like that it has different options you can choose from" - also shared that the options themselves seemed distinct.
- Would choose "exploration" "just to see what's out there."
    - Interesting that she interpreted "exploration" as potentially a mix of different styles, vs. a specific style of conversation.
- When shown the example conversational copy, she seemed a little confused. I asked if she'd expect to have to answer Ash's question, and she said "yes."
    - Is there a way to make it clearer that this example copy is static/for the sole purpose of showing users what the ensuing conversation might feel like?

### Perspectives screen

- "I like this part of it because I feel like it's going to personalize to me, and not just be a general thing."
- Seemed drawn to the feeling-related options over and above the others - "stress, loneliness, whatever it may be."
- Would expect the conversation to be tailored more towards the topic she chose.
- After glancing over the options a second time, she also shared that she'd be drawn to the parenting perspective.
    - What would you want out of a parenting-oriented conversation, I then asked?
        - "Just to listen to me… to hear me out about being a single parent. Any direction, any feedback at all… some days I feel like I'm pulling my hair out. I'd just want somebody to talk to about everything."
        - Perhaps it will be helpful to continue to ask users about their expectations re: perspectives/lenses as we continue to iterate on the prompts!

### Personality screen

- "Again, I love that it lets you choose."
- "It's not too busy. The interface is not overwhelming… you don't have a million different things to choose from. It's simple and to the point."
- Observed that all of the categories seemed pretty distinct.
- Would likely choose "warm" and "laid back - "I'm very warm and very caring, but I'm laid back and can go with the flow"
    - Interesting to note that some users choose personalities/tones that feel and sound more like them, while others choose tones that are more distinct/diametrically opposed to who they are.
- Would you expect to be able to pick multiple personas?
    - "Depends on the day."
    - Like the other user I interviewed today, she seemed to exhibit a need for different tones/orientations depending on what she needs help with. Even if it's fully customized, one single persona might not meet the user's needs in every situation! A sign that we should make switching/editing as seamless as possible, maybe?

### Topics screen

- The user exhibited a markedly less positive/enthusiastic response to this screen vs. the others.
    - "These are your hobbies and whatnot… it's not really something I'd come on here to talk about. Of course I love to read, I love to watch movies, I love to do all these things. But I guess I would use the app more as… not just to talk about hobbies, or whatever.
    - Then she said something REALLY interesting: "I would expect a screen like this if I was on a dating app and if I was looking for a companion."
        - How does that make you feel? I then asked.
            - "Just like I would be on any other app… I would not expect this screen because I've used Ash and I know how good it is."
            - Fascinating that she sees us as being distinct from/in a different kind of ballpark than her other apps, and that this particular customization screen made her feel less that way - more like she was "on Pinterest" or another consumer app.
    - As we wrapped up the interview, she shared that she has pretty much no constructive feedback - "it's the best thing ever that it's free and it really feels like you're talking to a person. It doesn't feel robotic… it feels like I'm talking to a friend."
        - I then asked a bit of an (admittedly leading) question, which is: would the topics screen make it feel more or less friend-like and authentic?
            - She immediately answered "less," saying: "it throws me off a little."
            - This is super important I think!!! I have talked to many people out there who have authentic, productive relationships with Ash, while retaining an awareness that it is an AI. If we try to give it overtly human qualities, will we upset that delicate balance and actually make it harder for them to build a rapport?
