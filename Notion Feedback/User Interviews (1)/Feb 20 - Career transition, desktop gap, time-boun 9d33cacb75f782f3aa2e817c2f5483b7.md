# Feb 20 - Career transition, desktop gap, time-bound feature idea

Interview Date: February 20, 2026
Interviewer: Clare Flanagan
Key Themes: Comparison to therapy, Pacing feedback, UX copy feedback
Paid v. Organic: Paid
Primary Persona: N/A
User Description: Woman going through a career transition and recovering from a toxic work environment. Found Ash via an NYT article. Uses Ash alongside an in-person therapist and career coach, primarily for in-the-moment support. Heavy typer who has been using Ash less due to lack of a desktop interface.

## Background & Why She Started Using Ash

- Found Ash through an NYT article about "AI therapeutic instruments" — "I was like ooh, this could be very interesting."
- Searching for support during a career transition and recovery from a toxic work environment.
- Uses Ash alongside an in-person therapist and career coach — "nice to have in her back pocket."

## Ash vs. Therapy

- "AI is really good for helping me refine questions or tools that I've built up with actual people… it works really well when you can ask it good questions."
- Uses AI to process books/articles she's reading and reflect on how they apply to her life.
    - Example: highlighted notes from a book, dumped them into ChatGPT, and asked it to pull references and connect them to her thinking — something a therapist can't do.
    - Note: Ash likely can do this too — potential opportunity for onboarding/"how to use Ash" content.
- This practice helps her come to in-person sessions prepared with questions and focus areas.
- Would be interested in sharing Ash conversation snippets with her therapist or coach: "Yes, that would be really useful."
- On the difference between Ash and therapy: "It's a lot more iterative with AI… a lot more ruminative. Whereas therapists have a lot more intent… if you're working with AI, they have a tendency to support the path you're going down."

## Persona Feedback

- Only tried the feature a couple of times but liked it.
- The persona feature helped her conceive of a new way to use Ash — she started a voice conversation while walking home after drop-off.
    - "When it said there was a persona, I thought maybe I can talk to it in the way I would talk to a person on the phone."
    - Insight: personas may open users up to capabilities or interaction styles they hadn't considered.
- Typically types, but found voice mode useful for dumping thoughts while on the move.

## Desktop & Interface Feedback

- Has been using Ash less lately partly due to lack of a desktop interface.
    - Types a lot and copies/pastes from other sources — mobile-only is inconvenient.
    - "I wish Ash had a desktop interface so I could say more to it."
- Interface changes on login are distracting: "I get distracted by the new interface with a lot of different options, whereas with ChatGPT, the only thing you can do is type."
- Reluctant to invest time figuring out a complex product.

## Feature Complexity & Pacing

- Paths/activities make Ash feel like "it's moving more towards something where you're going to spend an hour on it" vs. the in-the-moment support she values.
- "Sometimes in the middle of the workday I just need someone to tell me it's all going to be okay."
- Suggested a **brief check-in mode** for quick, immediate support.
- Finds Ash goes on too long — wants more time-bound conversations.
    - "It would be nice if you could say I have time for a two-minute chat and it will make you feel like you've reached the end of it and you can just move on."
- Was surprised to learn she can direct Ash to end a conversation or work within a time constraint — suggests a clear opportunity to better educate users on how to shape their own Ash experience.

## Final Thoughts

- "It's been amazing. It's been a huge part of my recovery process to have this incredible therapeutic voice in my pocket… having it accessible for free has been transformative."
- Main shift: "Being able to have a reminder that I'm okay."
