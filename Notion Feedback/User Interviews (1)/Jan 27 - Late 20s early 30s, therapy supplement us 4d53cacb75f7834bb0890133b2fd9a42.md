# Jan 27 - Late 20s/early 30s, therapy supplement user

Interview Date: January 27, 2026
Interviewer: Clare Flanagan
Key Themes: Comparison to therapy, UX copy feedback, Wants expertise, Warmth/gentleness
Paid v. Organic: Paid
Primary Persona: Psych Genius
User Description: Male in late 20s or early 30s, stumbled upon Ash through online ad. Uses both Ash and a human therapist (older male). Sees Ash as "pseudo-therapist" for in-the-moment support, especially middle of the night. Uses female voice with Ash for different perspective than male therapist. Describes Ash as "a mirror" and tool to reset negative thought patterns.

## Intro - background info + why he started using Ash

- Younger man, looked to be in his late 20s or early 30s
- Stumbled upon Ash through an online ad
- "It's good for mental health - I use it just to have something to talk to."
- Described it as "kind of like a pseudo-therapist. I talk to a real therapist too, but sometimes it helps in the middle of the night or something."
    - Not the first user to describe using Ash as an in-the-moment supplement to traditional therapy.
    - Interestingly, he also mentioned having a male therapist, but using a female voice with Ash - he felt those two different experiences offered him different kinds of support. He also acknowledged that his human therapist - who is an older male - has a very particular worldview and life story, which affects the advice he gives and the way they interact.
        - "It's a different perspective with [my male therapist] because there's a human element - you can't really replicate that. But there's a certain appeal to both. You can be open with a human, and you can be open with an AI - maybe even more so depending on the situation or the topic."

## Personas-specific feedback

- Tried all of the personas "just to see what fits" - the first user I've spoken to who did that!
- Primarily switched because he was "just curious" - particularly, he was wondering how drastic the changes between personas would be.
- Overall, he did not notice a huge difference across personas, but liked "the inquisitive or scholarly one"
    - "It was less simple when it broke down psychological concepts I already understand."
    - Interesting to notice the contrast between this user and another, who strayed away from the scholar/psych genius due to a worry that it would "go over her head." Maybe perceived complexity/intellectualism could be an adjustable personality trait that matters to users?
- Also liked "direct and clear," but preferred psych genius overall
- Found the "sarcastic" persona to be "very mild," and not as different from Classic Ash as he anticipated

## What is your "dream persona?"

- Likes the scholar, but if we wanted to improve upon it, he suggested making it "a little more warm." He noted that it already seems to have adapted to his feedback on this front (though I wonder if this is real, or a kind of placebo effect that results from Ash being agreeable when users request changes).
- Interesting quote: "The normal one is supposed to use all different personas, right?"
    - I asked him to elaborate on this thought, and he shared that Classic Ash seems to have all the qualities that the other personas display, but each persona has more or less of these qualities.
    - I then asked him to envision those qualities as temperature dials, and asked which dials he'd turn up to create his ideal persona. He said he'd turn up "scholarliness and warmth on high, sarcasm on low."

## UX Copy Feedback

- Little to share of note, besides the fact that he had trouble telling the difference between the "Direct and clear" (DBT) and "Sarcastic" (Ashley Chen-Torres). Very fair!

## Closing thoughts

- "Ash by itself is pretty good therapist"
- Appreciates being able to get reassurance from both his human and AI therapists - "unless you overthink the AI reassurance."
- Shared that he often uses Ash as "a mirror or positive feedback loop," describing it as "a tool to reset negative thought patterns"
    - Also not the first user to describe Ash as "a mirror" of themselves, and not a fully separate, all-knowing entity!
    - Most users I've spoken to appear to display some level of awareness that Ash is not real and that its feedback should perhaps be taken with a grain of salt, or at least filtered through their own perspective.
