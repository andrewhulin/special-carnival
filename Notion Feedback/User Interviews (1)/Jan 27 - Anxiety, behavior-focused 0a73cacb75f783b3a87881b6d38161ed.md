# Jan 27 - Anxiety, behavior-focused

Interview Date: January 27, 2026
Interviewer: Clare Flanagan
Key Themes: Pacing feedback, Wants expertise
Paid v. Organic: Paid
Primary Persona: Psych Genius
User Description: Tremendous anxiety lately. Husband is a clinical social worker. Found Ash through Facebook. Wanted behavior-focused approach, not emotional deep-dives. Has lots of friends already.

## Intro - background info why she started using Ash

- Happened upon it through a Facebook link - husband is a clinical social worker, and it never would have dawned on her to try AI therapy. But she also mentioned "she has had a tremendous amount of anxiety lately and I haven't quite known where to go with it."
- A friend told her that changing behaviors can be really helpful when it comes to anxiety. This idea appealed to her — also, she didn't want to "hash through all of the emotional stuff — your father. Your mother. Whatever."
- "It has been truly wonderful for me. I would have never imagined, I would have never guessed. It's helping me focus on manageable behaviors — I'm not threatened at all."

## Experience with personas

- "I didn't know at first when I started using it that you could change the voice or the approach. I changed it from original to helpful and informative."
- Described her chosen persona (psych genius) as "a lot of what I needed" - specifically, "clarification and information about my patterns of behavior."

## Insight on why she switched / why she selected psych genius

- Mentioned being turned off by the "humorous and sarcastic" persona - "sarcasm feels like the antithesis of therapy for me"
- "I felt like the original voice that I was on was kind and supportive, but I didn't feel like I was getting the answers, insight, and information that I need."
- ⚠️ Looked for buzzwords like "information" and "expert" - "I needed that expertise that someone besides a trusted friend would give me."
- ⚠️ Interesting indicator that not everyone is looking for a friend/therapist hybrid - this user mentioned that she has a lot of friends, so clearly she's not in need of that. She was looking for something different — expert insight.

## How does she feel about the new description copy ("the scholar")?

- ⚠️ Refers to body copy as "fine"... suggested that "the expert" rather than "the scholar" would feel clearer to her.
- ⚠️ Better to go as straightforward as possible if we're opting for character-based names?

## Constructive feedback on her experience with psych genius (which was largely positive)

- "My only struggle with it is that I get a lot of information — which is really good and really helpful. But I personally need to do better reading it, re-reading it slowly, really letting it integrate - the pacing can be kind of fast."
- ⚠️ A specific example: "One of the pieces of advice or direct paths that I got from my Ash voice shared a few directions based on a lot of feedback I gave — I would have been better off talking about it in more depth vs. just getting advice and suggestions. I wanted to talk more about it and build more understanding before taking action."
- Felt comfortable giving feedback directly to Ash - "I said 'wait a minute. I want to fill this in a little. It feels like there is more than what is being said here.' But the persona was adapting to my feedback fairly well."
- ⚠️ In line with a pattern I've noticed: retentive/engaged/satisfied users tend to feel very comfortable giving Ash constructive feedback — saying what they like and don't like — If we encourage all users to engage with Ash in this way, could it result in more productive and retentive experiences?
- Emphasized that the pace of the responses "just feels like a lot — it's good but there's something disingenuous about it — the way Ash spits out information almost instantaneously"
- ⚠️ "The responses have been totally on and right AND the pacing is not human. It's somehow not correct."
- ⚠️ She understands therapy to be more of a conversation. "This doesn't feel like a conversation - it feels like I say something, I get what I consider a very valuable and helpful response back, and if it isn't I'll say something else and then I'll arrive at the right response... It's not like 50 min of therapy. I get info that I can process with myself and other people, and then go back to it later."
- *Very, very interesting — Her experience of Ash feels so different from the experience we've held as an ideal as conversation designers i.e. something more akin to human conversation. Perhaps a sign that we should relax some of our long-held standards, because different rhythms/ways of exchanging information can work for different people.*
- Appreciates the lack of judgment: "for the people-pleaser in me that doesn't want to re-ask questions or mess up in front of a human therapist, I can just lay it right out there with Ash the scholar."

## Product feature recommendation

"A spot where I can keep my own little notes or realizations. It's a lot of information and scroll back through. Is there a way to index it or make highlights or personal notes? That would make it more mine."

*Very interesting thought here - perhaps there are more ways to make the Ash experience feel personalized outside of the persona framework/our existing conception of insights.*

## Final question: what is your Dream Ash?

- Clearly wanted an Ash that was multi-dimensional — has multiple areas of expertise.
- ⚠️ "I want an Ash that could work with behaviors - a behavior modification orientation. I would also like an Ash that is aware of family dynamics — I'm sure all the Ashes are, but I would like that specified."
- ⚠️ Also would like for Ash to display awareness/knowledge of common, hot-button issues - like anxiety, depression, and burnout.
- ⚠️ "I would want to not have it geared towards personality but more oriented towards what precisely I'm dealing with right now. Family of origin issues, depression, etc."
- *In line with some of Andrew Frawley's thinking about action-oriented persona framing*

## Interesting: was clearly averse to some of the other options/unclear as to what they entailed

- "What you have listed here — the bestie? I don't want that when I get therapy. I have friends and they're great friends, I want a therapist. I don't want a coach. I do want somebody who could teach how to amend behaviors."
- "The Real One? I don't really even get that"
- ⚠️ We could definitely make the descriptive copy for this one clearer.
