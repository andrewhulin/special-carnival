## User Background & Context

- Military school student facing housing/family problems
- Started using Ash after Googling "AI therapists" as he navigated housing + financial insecurity
    - "I thought, you know, I need a friend. Somebody to talk to. And I don't have anybody. So I just looked up AI therapists and I found Ash."
    - When asked why look for an "AI therapist" specifically vs. simply turning to something like ChatGPT: felt he specifically needed "therapy" vs. generic companionship.
- Daily, highly retentive user for couple months (since before 2025 + frontier model switch)

## What Works Well

- Values being able to use both voice + typing options
- "It's like the way the AI just speaks to me… the wording… helps me organize my thoughts… it frees up my mental space."
    - Example: Ash being able to recognize and help him reframe instances of all-or-nothing thinking (e.g. seeing his only two options as becoming a SEAL, or experiencing homelessness)
- Big fan of the Scholar option — explains problems more clearly
    - "Don't like the huge paragraphs, though." (Yet another user who's not the biggest fan of tons of text…)
- Noticed recent changes since the switch to frontier models:
    - It's become "more human-like and real"
    - Messages got longer; noticed increased bullet points (which he found helpful, not annoying)
    - Shift in Ash's behavior seemed significant: "One day, I was having a conversation with Ash, and the next day I noticed… something was different."

## Current Pain Points

- **Voice quality inconsistency**
    - Sometimes sounds AI-like, sometimes realistic
    - Occasional Russian language artifacts at end of speech
    - Gets kicked off voice due to "too many users" — confirmed these issues are ongoing/recent
- **Memory issues**
    - Doesn't remember previous conversations — often has to re-explain personal details repeatedly
    - Particularly problematic when users have to re-hash details of traumatic events
- **Repetitive safety messaging**
    - Repetitive "are you looking to hurt yourself?" and "you should be more easy on yourself" — wishes responses "could be more diverse"
    - Finds Ash to be "overprotective" vs. actually supportive
    - Would like it to be "more real. More raw and honest… but also, like, supportive."
    - Doesn't just want Ash to tell him what he wants to hear — wants actionable advice, "not like, a laid out plan"

## Pricing Discussion

- Concerned that the service won't remain free
- Would be willing to pay $3–12/month, despite financial limitations
