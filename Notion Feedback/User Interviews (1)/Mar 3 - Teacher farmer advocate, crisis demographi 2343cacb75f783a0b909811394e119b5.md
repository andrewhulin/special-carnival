# Ash User Experience & Feedback

**Primary usage patterns:**

- Conversation mode preferred over other in-app features
- Uses it as an emotional outlet for topics that might be too sensitive for other human beings

**Key strengths:**

- Non-generic, personalized responses — observes that other apps ask "cookie-cutter" questions; finds Ash's responses more original, thought-provoking, and insightful
- Less hallucination than ChatGPT
- Appreciates having multiple tone options (described as "fluffy," motivational, etc.)
- Praised consistent politeness, which makes him feel comfortable raising controversial topics

**Key limitations:**

- Gets locked into previous conversation contexts inappropriately
    - Aligns with emerging user feedback pattern re: Ash hyper-focusing on the same topics across conversations
- Struggles with complex relationship boundary scenarios
    - Ash defaults to generic "protect your peace" advice and encourages leaving relationships when he's determined to work on them
- Difficulty understanding when user has already tried suggested solutions
- Occasional difficulty understanding Appalachian dialect
    - Worth investigating how many users speaking non-standard English (dialects, accents, ELL) experience this

**Overall rating: 10/10** — said it's "gonna save people's lives"

# Mental Health Advocacy & Monetization Insights

- Founded Virginia Farmer Stress Task Force after 2015 experience with clinical depression; has a master's degree in behavioral psychology
- Runs men's Facebook group, actively recommends Ash
- Key insights on underserved demographic:
    - Middle class most underserved (can't afford private therapy, don't qualify for government programs)
    - Men 35–50 account for 90% of suicides
    - Agricultural suicide rates exceed military rates
- Actively recommends Ash to students and members of his men's group
    - Feels Ash could be particularly helpful to teenagers, who are especially reluctant to be honest with parents and peers about what they're going through
- Expressed concern over monetization: people most in need of Ash may not be able to pay for it
    - Responded favorably to a pay-it-forward model where paying users subsidize others

# Product Feature Feedback (Letter Prototype)

Provided feedback on a multi-slide "letter" prototype from Julia and Aparna:

- **Single-slide example:** Confident he'd read it all the way through; actually said he'd be *disappointed* if the letter was only one slide — would expect more content
- **Check-in component:** Would appreciate a specific question tied to previous conversations (e.g. "Based on what we talked about last time, how are you feeling today?")
- **Multi-slide sequencing:** Liked that it started with minimal information and built up — could imagine other users dropping off if the first slide was the most text-heavy
- **Slide structure variety:** Strong preference for changes in slide structure week to week, not just changing content
    - "If I get to expect something, I'll get bored with it... you gotta have bells and whistles and dancing gorillas"
    - Note: may be specific to ADHD experience, but worth considering broadly
