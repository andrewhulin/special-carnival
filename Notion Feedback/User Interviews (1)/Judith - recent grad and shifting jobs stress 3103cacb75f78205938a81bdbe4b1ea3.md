# Judith - recent grad and shifting jobs stress

Interview Date: February 26, 2026
Interviewer: Derrick Hull
Key Themes: Meta Ad
Paid v. Organic: Paid
Primary Persona: N/A
User Description: Judith had a human therapist, but switched insurance when switching jobs and didn’t have strength to go through hassle of finding one again. Tried Ash with low expectations, it blew her mind, she’s not sure she’ll ever do human therapy again

### **Judith’s Background & Ash Discovery**

- Based in New Jersey, works in healthcare
- Hobbies: gardening (recently grew white eggplant), has 3-year-old cat
- Discovered Ash through Instagram ad
- English is second language

### **Ash Usage Experience**

- Started before personas/journeys features added
- Uses 2-4 times per week, finds helpful ~80% of time
- Prefers typing input, hearing voice responses
- Finds AI easier than human therapy
    - No need to manage therapist’s reactions or emotions
    - Can directly correct/train AI without social concerns
    - Learns preferences surprisingly quickly
- Two main personas used:
    1. Scholar - for logical, factual responses without emotional exploration
    2. Bestie - for exploring feelings and sitting with emotions
    - Notes: personas don’t sound different enough, bestie still sounds scholarly
- Likes conversation history feature with suggested follow-up topics
- Uses journeys weekly but wants AI prompting/scheduling suggestions

### **Therapy Comparison & Value**

- Previously in human therapy, switched to Ash due to insurance changes/job transitions
- Prefers Ash overall but misses human elements:
    - Physical comfort (hugs, touch)
    - Natural empathy without having to request it
    - Less solution-focused approach by default
- Helped through job transition stress, feels calmer using it
- Plans to use “as long as app exists”

### **Pricing Feedback**

- Comfortable paying $15/month subscription
- Wouldn’t pay $99 upfront without trial (too risky for untested product)
- Suggests 30-day trial, then annual option
- After experience: would now pay $99/year or more
- Dislikes usage-based pricing, prefers per-session caps over word limits
