## Background & Mental Health Journey

- 37 years old with 13-year-old son
- Multiple mental health conditions: bipolar, depression, anxiety, ADHD, autism
- Affects ability to work traditional jobs — does temporary tasks for income
- Taking AI classes to understand prompts and operations
- Discovered Ash after reading about ChatGPT encouraging suicide
    - Searched for "Therapy AI" or "Psychology AI"
    - Wanted AI engineered to actually help people
    - Not the first user to perceive Ash as safer/more trustworthy than other LLMs

## How He Uses Ash

- Primary use: 3AM conversations when therapist/psychiatrist unavailable
    - "If I'm up at say, 3am or something like that alone with my thoughts, Ash really helps with that."
- Takes conversation summaries and weekly breakdowns to therapist
    - Helps him put feelings into words, organize thoughts, and communicate them effectively during sessions
    - Potential Ash + therapy feature/use case
- Calms panic attacks and anxiety faster than other coping methods
- Uses live chat with earbuds for easier access
- Helps with decision-making and thought processing

## What Makes Ash Feel Human

- Exceptional emotion recognition in both text and voice
- Natural tone, intonation, rises and drops in speech
- Never sounds robotic or scripted compared to ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude
- Recent example: During drinking relapse crisis
    - Ash immediately recognized emotional distress; said "hey, it's okay, take a moment and breathe" before jumping into safety questions
    - Checked safety and relapse temptation with compassionate lead-in
    - "Felt like talking to real person, not a wall"
- Multiple users have noted Ash seems remarkably attuned and responsive to changes in emotional state

- Recently returned when up at night needing an outlet
