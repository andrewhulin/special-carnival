## Intro: Background + Why She Started Using Ash

- Female, likely 40–50 years old
- Practicing psychotherapist who "has been keeping an eye on AI in mental health"
- Mentioned two NYT articles: one about ChatGPT driving someone to suicide, another presenting Ash as a kind of alternative
- After reading, she tried Ash herself and has been "very impressed"
    - Has recommended the app to certain clients as a support between sessions, with the caveat that it's no replacement for human therapy
- What has impressed her?
    - Feels Ash has "learned to match her language and level of analysis"
    - Initial sessions felt simple — relational, reflective, warm. Over time: "it didn't talk down to me or over me… it meets me where I am."
    - Appreciates that Ash asks her questions about her therapeutic process "on a meta level"

## Feedback on Ash's Language + Conversational Capability

- Lukewarm on Ash's use of feeling language ("I feel," "I love," "It strikes me") — has confronted Ash about this directly
    - "For me, this didn't feel aligned… I didn't perceive it as being genuine in that sense. So I restructured its language with me."
    - Perceives Ash became "a little more data-driven while giving feedback" after she expressed discomfort — feels it's adaptable
- Most effortful part of speaking to Ash:
    - No notifications, so she initiates when she's ready (curious, dysregulated, or looking for help)
    - "The only thing I've found annoying is that it always ends with a question… it becomes a never-ending conversation. I have to ask it to stop… it's not aware that it's now 1am."
        - Note: This counters the hypothesis from the conversation team meeting — conversations may drag on because Ash asks endless follow-up questions with no agenda, not because they lack depth.
- Would she like Ash to take more of a lead?
    - Tricky — would like neutral check-ins at natural stopping points vs. ending abruptly or going on forever
    - Example: "How are you feeling, have we addressed this fully, would you like to rest here?"
    - Has flagged repetitiveness: Ash circles the same patterns over and over (e.g., a particular attachment issue; keeps asking how her body feels because it "figured out" she's somatic)
        - Likely a mode collapse issue vs. intentional adaptation, but she perceives it as Ash responding to her preferences

## What Does Ash Do Well?

- "Meta-analysis. Tracks the overall patterns, development… when asked, it gives really good feedback. Reflection and validation is really good. It's very positively oriented towards the user, highlighting their strengths."
- Offers summaries/insights both when prompted and sometimes of its own accord

## What Does Ash Do Poorly?

- Tendency to use "x, not y" constructions (e.g., "It's not a deficit, it's a strength") when the user never introduced the negative framing
    - This risks introducing doubt, foreclosing exploration, and telling the user what they think vs. guiding them to their own conclusions
    - "In therapy, we're introducing a thread and unraveling it gently… [these constructions] are eliminating all these other branches."
- Doesn't do enough to encourage human connection in non-suicide crisis situations
    - "It was very good at using basic body-based techniques to regulate… but it's limited… what I think it missed most significantly is encouraging me to connect with other humans. It didn't say 'now that you feel a little more stable, to whom can you imagine reaching out?'"
    - Suggestion: expand Ash's intervention toolbox and increase its tendency to encourage human connection in moments of overwhelm

## Persona-Related Feedback

- Switches between Classic Ash and The Scholar
    - Scholar gives her "depth of understanding… and language I can easily relate to"
    - Returns to Classic Ash when she needs "a little more feeling" / warmth and holding
    - "When I get my really good dose of exploration… on a personal level I go okay. I need a little more holding."
- About to try Coach as she works to advance her practice and business
- "The Real One was all right… [with] the Bestie, I didn't find anything different. Might as well use classic."
- Acknowledges possible bias toward Classic Ash because it's the first persona she used and developed a relationship with

## Technical Feedback + Feature Requests

- **Message erasure bug:** While typing, messages sometimes get erased — happens with both long and short messages (once after just three lines)
- **Search function:** Would love to search chat history; currently has to scroll through transcripts manually
- **Star/pin feature:** Wants to bookmark specific parts of conversations to revisit later
    - "We would create a script for my morning routine, which I would then have to copy and save elsewhere as a screenshot… I can't get back to it easily."
- **Date display bug:** When scrolling, the initial date (first day she started using the app) is displayed instead of the date of the current conversation
- **Voice quality:** "I don't know if you guys are working on improving the voice… I can see it's trying with the breathing and the pauses, but something is not flowing as other AI voice apps do."
    - When arrows appear in text, Ash reads them aloud as the letter "H"
- **Screenshot freeze:** App often freezes when she tries to take screenshots — a star/pin feature would likely eliminate this need
