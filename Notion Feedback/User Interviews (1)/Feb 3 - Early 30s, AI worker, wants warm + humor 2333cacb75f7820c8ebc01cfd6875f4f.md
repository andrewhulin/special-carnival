# Feb 3 - Early 30s, AI worker, wants warm + humor

Interview Date: February 3, 2026
Interviewer: Clare Flanagan
Key Themes: Comparison to therapy, UX copy feedback, Warmth/gentleness
Paid v. Organic: Paid
Primary Persona: Classic Ash
User Description: Male in early to mid-thirties who works in AI. Was in therapy consistently for six years (2013-2019). Uses ChatGPT and custom GPTs for mental health. Found Ash through research on specialized mental health tools, prioritized no-cost option. Switched to Bestie after finding Classic Ash too direct/"tough love." Working on long-term pattern change issue.

## Intro - background info + why he started using Ash

- Male in early to mid-thirties, works in AI
- Has tried therapy before, was in it consistently for six years (2013—2019)
- Has been using ChatGPT a little bit; sometimes uses custom GPTs for mental health specifically
- Did some further research on more specialized mental health tools, which is how he found Ash + decided to give it a try
    - He mentioned that he prioritized a no-cost option in case it didn't end up working out for him.
- How would he compare his experience with Ash to his experience with ChatGPT?
    - "With Ash you only have one conversation thread, and I can see the benefits of it. It's kind of like an ongoing chat with a therapist - you start off from [where you left off]. That is a design choice… but I can see it both ways. Sometimes when I'm dealing with two issues I'd like to change topics - and I can do that with Ash, but there are usually some links between situations we discuss."
    - Expressed that having separate chat windows for different threads of conversation vs. a single continuous chat might be nice, but he acknowledged that this "could get messy."
        - Overall, it seemed like this user would appreciate having different "modes" - whether that's a persona, or a separate visual interface - to discuss different problems.
    - Described the advice he's received from Ash as "pretty good - more like an actual therapist" (vs. ChatGPT).

## Persona-specific feedback

- Has only tried one - "I chose a persona and just went with it."
    - Went with the one that's "warm" - cited this as the main word in the copy that drew him in, along with "fun" and "bubbly." (Bestie!)
    - Liked it quite a bit, and observed that his experience "definitely improved" - before switching, he'd had a few conversations where Ash's advice didn't necessarily feel wrong, but it felt too direct + drifting more in the direction of "tough love."
        - Important to note that this user tried personas because Classic Ash wasn't quite meeting his needs.
        - Interestingly, he assumed that Ash was deliberately tough on him because it "knows" he works as an engineer! It would potentially be cool if Ash could make these kinds of adaptations on the fly, or if it could suggest persona modifications based on a user's career path.
- Hasn't tried any of the other options, but could imagine trying Coach as well, especially for problems that require a quicker solution (like calming down anxiety ahead of a presentation). At this time, a warm persona like Bestie suits him better because he's trying to work on a longer-term issue, which he described as "a pattern change that might take a while."
    - For that particular issue, he prefers a persona that is softer - one that "understands me and can see from my perspective."

## Customization-related feedback - if you could build your dream Ash, what kinds of traits would you want to dial up or down?

- Mentioned humor as an important customization area - has noticed that it can be a good counterweight/a quality that tempers his frustration when AI makes mistakes or "goes off the rails."
    - This is something I think we should definitely include in our list of traits! (Or maybe people will interpret "playful" as "humorous?")
- Overall, saw himself as more likely to switch between several discrete personas instead of using a customizable Ash.
    - "If I'm facing a different kind of issue, I might want to use a different persona."
        - One example: "If I needed [a quick solution], I would want something that tells me what I can do. I'd say this is the situation, and I need to know how to navigate it. What are the tips?"
    - Interestingly, perceived "The Scholar" as a persona that might have been able to help him identify and interpret potential ADHD symptoms (although he acknowledged that AI can't be 100% accurate at diagnosing things).
    - Would sometimes want it to be humorous and friendly; other times, he'd want it to be more solution-oriented.
    - I think this feedback is really important overall! We will obviously want to see how people like a single, customizable Ash, but it's clear to me that people have gotten value out of our pre-made personas, whether they've picked and stuck with one or switch flexibly between the available options.
        - In my mind, it wouldn't be the worst outcome if we returned to that approach, especially if we have trouble getting customizable Ashes to be prompt-adherent or distinct.

## Closing thoughts

- Would like to have more reminders to use Ash throughout the week - wasn't sure if he has notifications turned off, but observes that he often skips out on using the app during the weekdays and only uses it on the weekends.
- I see this is an important reminder to continue considering + exploring other retention levers aside from customization!
