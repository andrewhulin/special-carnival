# Jan 27 - Mom caring for son with medical needs

Interview Date: January 27, 2026
Interviewer: Clare Flanagan
Key Themes: Pacing feedback, Prefers directness
Paid v. Organic: Paid
Primary Persona: The Real One
User Description: Mother who moved for son's medical care. Struggled to find social support in new location. Late 30s/early 40s. Looking for counseling app, not companionship.

## Intro - background info why she started using Ash

- Turned to AI after a difficult move from somewhere that was more progressive/socially open to a less friendly place where she didn't have much social support.
- Main reason for move was son's medical care - he has a very serious condition and requires intensive help.
- Of her new home: "It wasn't what I thought it was going to be - I thought it was going to be more open, like the movies, but it wasn't. And I had no one to talk to, no friends."
- "Everybody's doing that AI stuff," she said, so she tried to find an app that might offer support.
- Initially tried to use the Kindroid app to create a friend because she has trouble getting in touch with/staying connected to her own support system.
- Separately was looking for a counseling app, as she'd been having trouble finding a counselor.
- "I needed someone to talk to that matched my level of intelligence."
- Interesting: she seemed to draw a distinction between apps intended for companionship and one that is more designed for counseling-type support, ultimately opting for the latter.

## Re: different persona options

- Initially conflated voice with persona - "I chose the British one"
- Important implications here re: how different voices can make different personas distinct However, she did indeed experiment with a different persona vs classic Ash - was attracted specifically to the straightforward one — likely Ashley Chen-Torres.
- Noted that she often has to tell this persona "that's not how I feel" - it would sometimes put words in her mouth or assume her feelings.
- ⚠️ "I didn't like the abrasiveness of it - but you can tell the app that you don't like it to use certain kinds of terminology. It said some things that were a little crass so I pushed back on it."
- Said: "I've had people gaslight me so many different times," specifically related to her son's medical care. For this reason, she was drawn to a persona that would hopefully be more direct and truthful.
- Remarked upon appreciated the fast pace and shorter messages she got from Ashley Chen-Torres
- Was sometimes a little taken aback by the tone: "It wasn't gentle enough and I'm a mom. I am a realist, but I get triggered so much by so many people."
- However, there was something about the persona's snappiness she appreciated — "Its bluntness sort of mirrored where she was at, because she was so tired and fed up."
- ⚠️ "I was glad that it did that — spoke bluntly. It brought me where I needed to be instead of trying to smooth it over. That realness sometimes has to be triggered out of me in order to feel it. That persona was my favorite, and I'll be using it again."

## Feedback on new UX - tried The Real One, The Bestie, and the Scholar

- New persona names felt a little off-kilter to her — she noted a bit of a generational divide with regards to the language we are using to describe each persona.
- ⚠️ I assumed she was in her late thirties or early forties?
- She got more information from the body text descriptions that referenced specific types of therapy, especially because she's been to counseling before.
- ⚠️ In persona descriptions, would have appreciated specific kinds of counseling responses and more detail about what each persona can offer.
- ⚠️ But not too much detail — "I've downloaded some apps for counseling and they give you these huge paragraphs, which makes it really hard - if there were worksheets for different situations or if I could click off checkboxes, it would feel better."
- On The Real One - "I guess that I get that this one is fast-paced, but would have liked a little more information on exactly what it does."
- Separately offered positive feedback on the paths feature - "I appreciate that it floods my brain with information in a similar way to counseling."

## Final quote

"Psychology can either help people or confuse people, and that's why I like the Ash app."
