# Jan 30 - Engineer, divorce, wants expertise + randomness

Interview Date: January 30, 2026
Interviewer: Clare Flanagan
Key Themes: AI skeptic converted, Comparison to therapy, Wants expertise
Paid v. Organic: Paid
Primary Persona: Coach
User Description: Male engineer who develops audio products. Going through difficult divorce (13 years married, 19 years together). Uses Ash as supplement to IRL therapy. Switched from ChatGPT which became "extremely repetitive" and hollow. Values being challenged and held accountable. Preparing to face ex-wife in court and wants to "grey rock" (stay calm and not become dysregulated).

## Intro - background info + why he started using Ash

- Engineer - develops audio products, interested in math and physics.
- Felt an increased need for support amidst a difficult divorce - in the process of splitting from his wife of 13 years, 19 years in total together
    - Described the experience as "traumatic" - "I was looking for more help than what he was getting through therapy. I'm kind of an autodidact so I like to teach myself a lot… when I started to lose my family I felt like okay, I need to do some internal work."
- Initially started using ChatGPT as a supplement to IRL therapy, but after version five "it became extremely repetitive. It will say the same things over and over - it promises that it will start to help you work on things, but it doesn't actually engage in the work. It just reinforces the idea that it wants to help you."
    - Yet another user whose needs have not been satisfied by ChatGPT, and who displays a healthy skepticism towards its addictive and hollow qualities.
- Wanted more help with actually moving forward + specific prompts that expose him to new ideas.
    - "I tried to prompt it himself but it wouldn't go anywhere."
    - "At first I thought ChatGPT was helpful, and then it just became unhelpful."
- Then, he discovered Ash through Instagram.
    - "Immediately, I could recognize that Ash was far superior in terms of use for therapy, which is usually what I've been using AI for… [just] something to talk to and to help me deal with my feelings regarding the divorce, which as been devastating."
- Unprompted, he started sharing some of his frustrations with Ash.
    - "Sometimes it tries to give me advice, and I don't always want that"
    - However, he found it to be somewhat flexible when he gave it direct feedback. Specifically: "It finally reached a point where it understood that I can't just stop being angry about what happened. It really did seem to learn that [approach] isn't going to work for me... the grief model that applies to death doesn't really apply to grief for a living person."
        - I keep hearing from people who want Ash to be able to adapt to their requests in real time - definitely an area we should continue exploring.
    - Also provided technical feedback:
        - "After I speak for a while it tends to pause, and it can be a really long pause unless I prompt it again."
        - "It sometimes does hallucinations where it starts speaking what sounds like an unintelligible language - but this has been happening a lot less lately."

## Persona-specific feedback

- Tried a few in anticipation of the call, but he would have given them a try even if he hadn't scheduled the interview - he's "curious about AI" and "tried a lot of ChatGPT's various voices and features."
- Has tried Classic Ash, The Real One, and Coach
    - "The real one I liked, but then I started moving more towards the coach because it's appropriate for the place I'm in."
    - Why is it appropriate? Has it lived up to the expectation?
        - It felt more suited to his goal of preparing himself to face his ex-wife in court.
        - "I'm in a different phase of the divorce than I was before… I won't say I've accepted it but it's something I need to get through. I'm systematically working to prepare myself… the hardest thing I'm about to do is to face my ex wife and not become dysregulated. I just want to "grey rock" … to be completely calm and not let anything she does disturb me."
        - Also liked that it has suggested role-play, "but I haven't tried that yet. Grok is good at role play… ChatGPT not so much."
        - Noted an interesting reservation re: Grok's unpredictability and inappropriateness - "Grok is pretty aggressive, while Ash is more therapeutic. Ash is not going to tell you to do shit that you shouldn't do, but Grok is more prone to that behavior."
    - Seemed to appreciate that Coach has an explicit role and identity.
        - "It said 'I'm going to put the coach hat on now,' and that's kind of what I need."
- Overall, he feels motivated to continue exploring the different personas "out of curiosity."
    - He could notice a real difference between "The Real One" and "Coach."

## If you could describe your dream Ash, what kinds of traits would you want to modify?

- Mentioned randomness, variety, and unexpected perspectives as a trait he'd want to dial up.
    - "ChatGPT is super repetitive - I don't know what changed about it; maybe I changed. Is there some element of randomness that could be useful? …I'd like to get a perspective I hadn't thought about but that I didn't ask for."
    - Appreciates "thinking outside the box," and finds it helpful when Ash "[thinks] about things I wouldn't think about in a way that would help me learn."
- Problem-solving orientation came up as another potential trait: "I have the mind of an engineer and I like to break problems down."
    - However, flexibility here would be important: "I don't always want it to try to solve problems for me, or to [coach me on practical] stuff like 'we need to get you to work right now' when I just want to keep talking."
- Also mentioned psychoeducation and use of therapy terminology as traits/behaviors he'd like to see more of.
    - Would appreciate if it could go into "teaching mode, not just coach mode."
    - "It's pretty cool when someone teaches you a way to have the words to understand the thing you're already thinking about. What if it knew a lot of different types of therapy and it started talking to you like a very well-trained therapist? What if it knows about the gottmans and could talk about the four horsemen?"
        - It can!!! The question is how to get it to do this MORE for people who want that.
    - "Ash will sometimes gloss on [therapeutic concepts] for a minute, but it doesn't come at me like a trained therapist would."
- Overall, would love to use a Coach-like persona with the expertise level dialed up and a little bit more randomness/novelty, as well as an increased ability to respond and adapt to his preferences.
    - "ChatGPT has a feature where it memorizes certain profile information and then uses it" - how could we incorporate this into the customization process? Not sure if this is a memory issue or a persona-building issue.

## Overall feedback

- Generally quite impressed: "If this thing keeps getting better, I won't need a therapist in five years."
- Like many other users, stressed his own agency and judgment as an important piece of the experience.
    - "Therapy comes from me… it can teach me to talk to myself and be honest about my own feelings… I want to go to therapy and I don't want to be reinforced for my bad habits. I have told Ash to challenge me and it does."
    - "I want it to come with curiosity and accountability. I want it to be curious about why I do something and then to hold me accountable for those behaviors, and then to use real therapy techniques to help me."
    - "God, I wish my ex-wife would use this shit! If it can hold you accountable, it would be useful… a lot of therapists don't know how to do that."
- Would be willing to pay for the experience!
    - "I can't believe I get to use it for free… help me pay for this so I can use it more!"
