# Feb 20 - Instant clarification, voice-first, therapy complement

Interview Date: February 20, 2026
Interviewer: Clare Flanagan
Key Themes: Comparison to therapy, Pacing feedback, Voice/audio feedback
Paid v. Organic: Paid
Primary Persona: N/A
User Description: Busy finance professional who uses Ash primarily for in-the-moment thought clarification, not structured learning. Sees a therapist but values Ash for its immediacy and mental-health-focused memory. Highly efficiency-oriented.

## How She Uses Ash

- "I use standard generative AI like ChatGPT to chat, but Ash is different - it allows me to pause and think in a way that's more true to human interaction."
- Uses Ash "quite often" but doesn't use every function — primarily uses the main chat.
- Has noticed new features like Pathways but doesn't find them valuable for her use case.
    - "The main reason why I turn to Ash is to have some kind of clarification of my thoughts or some kind of third party view. I only use it when I have an issue… 'pathways' sounds more like a tutorial. That's not my goal or my reason for using the app. I don't feel like I want to take a lesson, it's more I want instant clarification when I have issues."
    - Note: echoes feedback from another user interviewed the same day who found new features complex and assumed they'd be time-intensive.

## Voice Feedback

- Primarily uses voice mode and sees it as one of Ash's main advantages.
- "I didn't mind if it was a female voice or a male voice or whatever. I think the value of Ash is that it sounds like a human who is listening and talking."
- "The value add of Ash is the voice… it leaves a stronger impression on me than written text."
- Reported a few voice mode glitches:
    - After saying "thank you" to end a conversation, Ash sometimes says random/nonsensical things.
    - Ash sometimes reads out "dash" instead of pausing.

## Ash vs. Therapy

- Describes Ash as "like a replica of my therapist… except with Ash I don't have to book an appointment or find a slot that works for both of us."
- Still sees a therapist but has a busy schedule. "I want things to be sorted the moment they happen."
- Ash's views impact her thinking beyond her internal monologue — affects how she talks with other people too.
    - Example: Ash pointed out she was too results-focused at the expense of being present, which she recognized as a contributor to her mental instability.
- Values Ash's mental-health-focused memory vs. ChatGPT, which conflates work and personal topics.
    - "Ash is like a well of content that I generate, and share only about my personal well-being or mental health… it's long-lasting, a narrow focus, and it's an instant thing."
- Sees therapist for "overall weather patterns"; uses Ash for "whether or not the sun is shining on a given day."
    - Relies on human therapist for longer-term pattern analysis; Ash is for immediate, in-the-moment issues.
- "Also AI therapy is kind of new. We still have more trust in human beings."
- Hasn't thought about sharing Ash conversations with her therapist — views them as serving different purposes.

## Weekly Insights

- Doesn't use weekly insights often due to time pressure and efficiency focus.
- Conversations can be extremely long and cover many topics; doesn't feel efficient to revisit.
- Open to using it more because she wants the work to have a long-term impact, but time is a real barrier.
- Note: places a much higher premium on efficiency and instant gratification than most users interviewed.
