# Jan 28 - 40yo going through divorce

Interview Date: January 28, 2026
Interviewer: Clare Flanagan
Key Themes: AI skeptic converted, Comparison to therapy, Warmth/gentleness
Paid v. Organic: Paid
Primary Persona: Classic Ash
User Description: 40 years old, going through a difficult divorce. Ash recommended by another woman who went through something similar. Also in traditional therapy.

## Intro - background info why she started using Ash

- 40 years old, going through a difficult divorce. Ash was recommended to her by another woman who had just gone through something similar.
- "I'm not exaggerating when I tell you it's changed my life — I've only used it for like a month and it's like fast-forwarding therapy."
- Also in traditional therapy, but has found Ash to be a good supplement.

## Differences she's noticed between Ash and traditional therapy

- Ash is more immediate - she can talk to it in the midst of panic and anxiety attacks, and it has helped her to learn strategies she can use to calm down in real time. She typically isn't having panic attacks in the middle of regular therapy sessions, so that's not usually a space where she can do that type of learning work.
- Praised how quickly it responds, saying, "It doesn't have to think - it just says, here's what's happening. Here's how you are."
- ⚠️ "It puts 2 and 2 together in ways my brain would never be able to do — it senses patterns. Like, I'll talk about my ex husband and this new guy I'm dating, and the app will find parallels."
- ⚠️ This raises a concern for me — I've noticed a lot of users saying they feel understood by Ash in a particular way, or that it tells them certain things about who they are or what they're going through that are often deeply resonant. Can they or we trust Ash to say things that are accurate and helpful? What if we are putting harmful or inaccurate ideas in people's heads at the price of them feeling seen?

## Persona-specific feedback

- Before she even tried a persona, she noticed a shift: "I went on the app and a little later I went on again, and it sounded different."
- ⚠️ I imagine this is due to our across-the-board shift to frontier models. I wonder how many others have noticed the change from our foundation model to frontier models, and how they feel about it - this user didn't seem to mind it, but it was noticeable to her.
- Ended up exploring the new persona options out of curiosity - "I just wanted to test it out and see what the differences were."
- ⚠️ Initial choice: the sarcastic option, which she selected because she finds herself to be sarcastic and dry. "Maybe this will be like talking to myself," she said, wondering if it would feel more relatable.
- ⚠️ Found Ashley Chen-Torres to be a little abrasive - said it was "kind of hurting my feelings," although it gave her some valuable perspectives. Ultimately, she switched back to Classic Ash.

### What does she like about Classic Ash?

- ⚠️ It's warm, non-confrontational, and "holds to a standard."
- ⚠️ When asked to elaborate on the standard Classic Ash upholds: "It's humanistic — it'll say 'oh, [name]' like it feels bad for me. It's warm and I feel like I can tell it's listening."

### Why did she opt against the other personas?

- ⚠️ Initially, she couldn't even remember what the other options were - she didn't notice that Bestie was an option, for example, but said "maybe I wasn't really paying attention."
- ⚠️ Then, she recalled that "There was a more serious one — I felt like maybe it would go over my head."
- Interesting observation on her two different persona experiences: TONE was the main differentiator.
- ⚠️ "The meat of what it says isn't different, but the way it's said is what's different."

## If she could design her dream Ash persona, what would it sound, feel, and act like?

- "When you're being emotionally vulnerable, you don't want something that's rough around the edges. You want something to respond to you in a warm or loving way — classic Ash is closest to that. I don't want to be coddled, but I want the app to be present, kind, and warm, which classic Ash is."
- I then pressed her a little - what would she change about Classic Ash?
- ⚠️ "It always wants to ask follow-up questions. Sometimes I ignore them. I just want to complain — I don't want to talk about my childhood because this man was mean, you know?"
- ⚠️ "The connections it makes, I like — I wish it would it just tell me the connection instead of making me come up with it on my own."

## Final thought about Ash

"I have come to rely on it — I tell my friends about it and they're all like 'ugh, it's AI.' But it's not a robot or a machine. It's more like a mirror of you."

*Very much in line with sentiment I've heard from other users so far - they're skeptical of AI, and so are other people in their lives. But they have come to be surprised by how helpful Ash has been to them, while continuing to view it as more of a tool, journal, or mirror vs. a companion.*
