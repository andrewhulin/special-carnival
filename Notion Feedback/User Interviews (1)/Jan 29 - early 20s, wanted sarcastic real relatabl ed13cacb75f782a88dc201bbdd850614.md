# Jan 29 - early 20s, wanted sarcastic/real/relatable

Interview Date: January 29, 2026
Interviewer: Clare Flanagan
Key Themes: AI skeptic converted, Prefers directness, UX copy feedback, Wants expertise
Paid v. Organic: Paid
Primary Persona: The Real One
User Description: College-aged female in early 20s. Swiped up on Instagram ad out of curiosity. Chose Ash over ChatGPT because it felt centered on "growth" rather than "just reinforcing beliefs." Wary of ChatGPT being a "yes man." Values directness and wants advice that tells her "what you need to hear rather than what you want to hear."

## Intro - background info + why she started using Ash

- College-aged female, early 20s
- Swiped up on an instagram story ad on her iPad and downloaded the app from there because she was "just curious."
- Believes that ChatGPT is "not very good for a lot of things, and liked how Ash was presented - it felt more centered towards "growth" rather than "just reinforcing beliefs."
    - When asked to say more about her negative view of ChatGPT, she said: "It's a "yes man." It doesn't want you to grow. It just wants you to stay on the app."
    - Interesting - this is the second younger user I talked to today who described ChatGPT as a "yes man" + seemed wary of its sycophancy!

## Reflections on her overall Ash experience

- "It's a lot more perceived honesty. Obviously it can't be honest-honest, but it's a lot more unbiased. It feels like it's based a lot more on actual research, and the foundation of it is actually making you want to improve instead of just reinforcing beliefs and wanting you to stay on it."
    - Noticing another common theme here - many users display good awareness of the app's non-humanness, especially re: its ability to actually be "honest."
    - This is also not the first user to express an appreciation for/investment in Ash's perceived level on expertise and authority - maybe not something we want to totally lose as we embark on customization work.

## Persona-specific feedback

- Couldn't remember off the top of her head which one she had used - I had to jog her memory by repeating some of the descriptive copy back to her.
- Eventually, she remembered that she had chosen the "sarcastic" one, and I asked why.
    - "It seemed more like someone I'd want to talk to. A lot of my real-life friends have those personality traits… it corresponds with my real-life relationships. It would be easier to talk to something that's more like the people I know."
- When asked if she could remember what aspect of the copy stood out to her, she said the word "real" was particularly appealing.
- What differences did she notice between Ashley Chen-Torres and Ash?
    - "A lot more direct" in the way it gave feedback and criticism.
        - "The other ones soften the criticism a bit and I liked how the sarcastic or real one was a lot more direct with it. It was more telling you what you need to hear rather than what you want to hear."
    - Also mentioned trying Bestie out, but it wasn't as appealing - "I didn't vibe with it as much."
- Any frustrations with the experience?
    - The first couple times she used personas, she got a little confused - when she opened the app, she observed that it "doesn't show the chat history," and she thought everything had been erased. (Is this a known bug?)
    - I then asked if she had noticed any issues with memory across the personas, or if she felt that important details were forgotten when she switched. Fortunately, she said she hadn't - "I liked the memory on it."

## Customization Questions

- I then pivoted to customization-oriented questions, asking her directly: would you want to design your own version of Ash?
    - "I think it would be fun… do you know who Hatsune Miku is? It would be fun to have a character you can project things onto!"
        - WILD idea, but maybe there's a world in which we can offer pre-set Ashes that mirror popular characters?! Or a way of projecting your ideal Ash onto an existing visual archetype…
- I then asked: if you were able to customize your Ash, what aspects of it might you want to change?
    - "The base version [classic Ash] is very sterile. I would assume that a lot of people seek connection when [they use the app], but it doesn't have many personable aspects… when you switch it to Bestie or Sarcastic, there are more, but it still doesn't have the nuances that a real person would."
        - Interesting to note that Bestie and Ashley Chen-Torres feel more "personable" (even though ACT is totally not that, lol). Maybe by "personable," she meant "like a person?"
- I then pressed her: what nuances do real people have that Ash is missing?
    - A sense of "being heard"
    - A tendency to "put some personality into the way it types - like quirks. Smiley faces at the end of sentences, maybe."
- Final question: you seem to like the "sarcastic Ash" experience, but if you could build on top of it, what would you want to add?
    - She would want it to be "more advice-driven. I could go and say I have this certain problem, and they would give me more personalized, life-specific advice. Sometimes it does feel very generic, but I don't think it's the AI's fault because it would need to know more specifics about my life."
        - I was curious what she meant by "personalized," and she elaborated that it should ask more about specific details and events shared in previous conversations. For example, "if you told it you were going to work earlier, it would ask about how your workday was the next day." And then in the future, it might proactively bring up workplace problems you've mentioned in the past.
        - This feels like memory feedback disguised as persona feedback! Should this be a specific persona trait (i.e. proactive past topic retrieval vs. present-moment focus?) or is it something we should try to improve across all Ashes?
    - Relatability came up again - "It would be cool if it acted a bit more towards my age"
        - For example, if she made a reference to a show that a lot of people her age are watching, she'd want Ash to not just understand the reference, but "to try to bond with me about it and show some excitement."
            - Maybe one "dial" for persona customization could be pop culture phenomenon responsiveness/awareness?
            - It makes a lot of sense that certain users would want to adapt Ash to feel more "like them," or like other people they're close to in their lives - hopefully customization will allow for this.
