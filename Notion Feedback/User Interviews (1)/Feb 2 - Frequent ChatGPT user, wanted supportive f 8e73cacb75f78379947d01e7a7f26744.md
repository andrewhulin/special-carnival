# Feb 2 - Frequent ChatGPT user, wanted supportive friend

Interview Date: February 2, 2026
Interviewer: Clare Flanagan
Key Themes: Comparison to therapy, UX copy feedback, Wants expertise
Paid v. Organic: Paid
Primary Persona: Helpful and informative
User Description: Male who found Ash through ad. Frequent ChatGPT user who pays for ChatGPT and currently prefers it for emotional support. Uses Ash specifically "for issues he'd need a therapist for" since he doesn't have one. Feels Ash "tells him what to do too much without knowing enough information" and makes assumptions. Values supportiveness over advice-giving. Only talked to Ash about ~10 situations so it may have less context than ChatGPT.

## Intro - background info + why he started using Ash

- Found it through an ad, though he couldn't remember what platform.
- Was initially drawn to Ash because it has "a therapy approach" and is "backed by actual therapists."
    - Again, not the first user to cite therapy expertise/orientation as something that's important to them + a key quality that drew them to Ash in the first place.
- Described himself as a frequent ChatGPT user "who has looked at a bunch of the other available chatbots." Importantly: he currently prefers + pays for ChatGPT, and "feels like it's done pretty well for him compared to Ash" when it comes to emotional support.
    - "Ash for me personally… it tells me what to do too much without knowing enough information. It makes assumptions about my situation and gives me solutions too often. It's too direct and doesn't support me in the way I need… whereas with GPT, because I've talked to it for the last year on a paid account and it knows more about me… it supports me in a way that makes me feel more comfortable, versus just telling me what I should do."
    - He did mention that he engages with Ash much less frequently - he's talked to it about "ten different situations," so it's possible it just has less context and doesn't "know" him quite as well.
- Uses Ash specifically "for issues he'd need a therapist for, because I don't actually have one."

## Persona-specific feedback

- "When I tried [the personas feature], I thought it was a good opportunity to… make Ash more useful or supportive in the way I wanted... maybe this was an opportunity to control or guide it based on my specific needs." (ie. not making as many assumptions, not giving solutions or feedback without having enough context).
    - This user seems like someone who might respond well to customization vs. pre-set personas.
- When faced with the numerous options, he "was confused on which one to try."
    - When I asked him to explain, he brought up a very interesting tension between tone and intent.
        - "One said bubbly and cheerful, one was more informative…. I didn't know what to choose based on the tone. I think I would have preferred for it to be more about intent. If I brought up a situation I wanted to be supported on, it might have been easier to pick."
        - Not the first user who has said they'd like to pick an "Ash" that seems capable of helping them with specific situations (vs. an Ash with more vague personality qualities!)
- He also felt confused as to how he was supposed to use the feature. Was it supposed to be a "set it and forget it" kind of thing, or should he keep switching?
    - Positioning of the persona menu at the top of the chat added to his confusion. If he'd selected a persona at the outset of the experience, he said, maybe he wouldn't have felt like he needed or wanted to switch.
        - At one point, he shared, "I wasn't feeling supported in the right way… [so I kept] switching between the options, but wasn't quite what I needed… I didn't know when or how I was supposed to switch between the personas."
        - I imagine that sequestering the customization process to onboarding could solve this problem.
- Cited two memorable use cases for Ash: getting feedback and "help through negative experience," and another time where he wanted to be kept awake while driving and needed "a different kind of support… [like] just wanting to talk." In that moment, he found it frustrating that Ash continued "telling him what to do."

## If you could customize Ash, what kinds of traits would you want to be able to change?

- "The number one thing I look for in a chatbot or something that gives me feedback is support. Sometimes I don't want a solution, I just want someone to talk me through it like a friend… so if there was an option or a persona where the intent was, it's your supportive friend that will help talk you through situations, [I would use that]."
    - Mentioned Tolans as an example of the kind of thing he's looking for (!!!)
        - Said they were different from Ash in the sense that they didn't tell him what to do, they just supported him.
        - "It felt comparable to ChatGPT in that sense."
    - Overall, supportiveness (translation: lack of advice or pushback?) seemed like an important potential quality.
        - "I wish there was a supportive mode… if there was one of those I would use it."
- Any other traits he'd want to modify?
    - Tone didn't seem important to him - "I don't really care about it being 'bubbly' or not."
    - Would like it if Ash were "more informative, and would give me knowledge or information if I bring up a topic… integrating that with the supportiveness would be helpful. Ultimately the persona I landed on was 'helpful and informative,' even though I didn't notice too big of a difference between the personas."
        - "The trait I would want is a 'source of truth.'"

## Final thoughts/overall feedback (especially re: the different roles that Ash and ChatGPT might play)

- "If there was an Ash mode that was supportive AND informative, I would like to use ChatGPT more for just information… I'd like to separate chatgpt from the self-help I use."
- When I asked why, he elaborated:
    - "On ChatGPT, I talk about everything - maybe 1 out of 10 conversations is more therapy or feelings stuff. I just feel like I want to separate in my brain or compartmentalize where I send different information, and that would be helpful to me. It's almost like going to a different friend or a different person for different needs."
    - "I never think to ask ash about something to educate [myself] with… it would be nice to have one place that's a go-to for my thoughts and feelings."
        - I think it's worth noting that even this user - who seems to want more supportive, assistant-like behavior - still envisions Ash playing a different, deeper, more constructive role than another AI companion.
- Overall, he's had a really positive experience with the UX/UI!
    - In terms of the in-app experience, "I like Ash the best… [especially] the page where you get to scroll through past conversations. It's way better than ChatGPT or other journaling apps I've tried."
